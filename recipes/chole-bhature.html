Chole-Bhature Recipe

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that combines spicy chickpea curry (chole) with soft, fluffy fried bread (bhature). It’s a hearty and flavorful meal often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch, especially in Punjabi cuisine. The chole are made by simmering boiled chickpeas in a rich, tangy gravy of tomatoes, onions, and aromatic spices, while the bhature are prepared from fermented dough and deep-fried until golden and puffed. Served together with onions, pickle, and a squeeze of lemon, Chole Bhature is a delicious and satisfying dish loved across India for its bold flavors and comforting taste.

Ingredients

  • For Chole (Chickpeas Curry)
    • 1 cup chickpeas (kabuli chana) – soaked overnight
    • 2 onions – finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes – pureed
    • 1 green chili – chopped
    • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 1½ tsp coriander powder
    • 1 tsp cumin powder
    • 1½ tsp red chili powder
    • 1½ tsp garam masala
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves – for garnish
    • (Optional: 1 black tea bag for darker color while boiling chana)
  • Ingredients for Bhature
    • 2 cups maida (all-purpose flour)
    • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
    • 2 tbsp curd (yogurt)
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • ½ tsp baking powder
    • ¼ tsp baking soda
    • Salt to taste
    • Water – as needed to knead
    • Oil – for deep frying
  • Steps to Prepare Chole Bhature:
    1. Step 1: Boil Chole – Soak overnight, pressure cook for 5–6 whistles with salt and optional tea bag, then drain.
    2. Step 2: Make Chole Masala – Sauté onions, add ginger-garlic, tomatoes, and spices. Add boiled chickpeas and simmer 10–15 mins.
    3. Step 3: Prepare Bhature – Mix all ingredients, knead soft dough, rest 2–3 hrs, roll and deep fry till golden.
